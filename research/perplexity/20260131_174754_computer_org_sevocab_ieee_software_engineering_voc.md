## Response

The **SEVOCAB database** is a publicly accessible vocabulary resource hosted by the IEEE Computer Society that maintains standardized definitions for systems and software engineering terms[2]. It serves as the primary tool for maintaining ISO/IEC 24765, the International Standard for Systems and Software Engineering Vocabulary[2].

SEVOCAB entries are **arranged alphabetically** and can consist of single words (such as "software"), phrases (such as "test case"), or acronyms[2]. The database is designed to be accessible to the public, with copyright permissions that allow users to copy definitions as long as the source is cited[2].

You can access SEVOCAB at **www.computer.org/sevocab**[2]. The database is periodically published as a formal International Standard that reflects a "snapshot" of the current vocabulary entries[2]. This approach encourages the use of ISO/IEC JTC 1 and IEEE systems and software engineering standards by making the definitions freely available[2].

The vocabulary is intentionally comprehensive, covering terms currently in use across the field of software engineering and related areas including computer architecture, storage, data management, and computer security[3].
