### 🔬 **Research Report: Epistemological Status of Software Engineering**
**Context:** `wave/docs/theory/` | **Tool:** `./pe discuss` | **Framework:** Standard Model of Code

#### 1. The NATO 1968 Conference (The Genesis)
*   **Vision:** The term "Software Engineering" was coined deliberately as a provocation, not a description of reality.
*   **Science vs. Craft:** The attendees (including Naur and Dijkstra) recognized that software production was a "black art" or craft. The vision was to transform it into an engineering discipline grounded in **theoretical science**, similar to how electrical engineering is grounded in physics/electromagnetism.
*   **Relevance to Project:** This is the origin of the "Crisis" that `PROJECT_elements` attempts to solve by formalizing the "constituents" (atoms) of code.

#### 2. Edsger Dijkstra (The Mathematical Purist)
*   **Position:** Software is a branch of **Mathematics**.
*   **Argument:** Programs are abstract mathematical objects. Correctness should be proven formally (like a theorem), not established by testing ("Testing shows the presence, not the absence of bugs").
*   **Consensus:** Dijkstra represents the "Rationalist" school.
*   **Relevance to SMC:** Your `particle/docs/proofs/` directory and `L0_AXIOMS` align with Dijkstra’s view. The "Collider" seems designed to automate the extraction of these mathematical properties.

#### 3. David Parnas (The Engineering Pragmatist)
*   **Position:** Software is **Engineering**.
*   **Argument:** Unlike pure math, software must deal with physical constraints (time, memory, legacy systems) and human limitations. He emphasized **Information Hiding** and modularity not just for mathematical beauty, but to manage complexity and enable parallel work.
*   **Education View:** He argued SE education should focus on "professional practice" based on solid principles, distinct from Computer Science (which is the study of computation, not building systems).

#### 4. Fred Brooks (The Essentialist)
*   **Position:** **Not a Science** (in the reductionist sense).
*   **Argument:** In *No Silver Bullet*, Brooks distinguishes between **Essence** (the abstract complexity of the problem) and **Accidents** (the labor of coding). He argues there is no "law of physics" discovery that will yield a 10x productivity gain because the complexity is inherent to the conceptual structure, not the medium.
*   **Relevance:** Your project’s **Wave** domain (Context) addresses the "Essence," while **Particle** addresses the "Accidents" (syntax, ASTs).

#### 5. Gang of Four (The Naturalists)
*   **Position:** **Taxonomy/Natural History**.
*   **Discovery vs. Invention:** The GoF (Gamma, Helm, Johnson, Vlissides) did not *invent* patterns; they *discovered* recurring structures in successful systems. This is akin to a biologist categorizing species.
*   **Consensus:** Patterns are "empirical observations" of solutions that resolve forces in a context.
*   **Relevance:** Your `particle/src/patterns/` directory and `atom_classifier.py` are automating this naturalistic discovery process.

#### 6. SEMAT (The Modern Theorists)
*   **Position:** **Theory-based Engineering**.
*   **Initiative:** "Software Engineering Method and Theory" (Ivar Jacobson, Bertrand Meyer).
*   **Goal:** To move SE from a collection of fads (Agile, Waterfall) to a discipline based on a "Kernel" of universals.
*   **Relevance:** Your `.agent/KERNEL.md` and `.agent/META_REGISTRY.yaml` mirrors the SEMAT goal of defining a "Kernel" of essential elements (Alphas) that exist in every project regardless of methodology.

#### 7. Lehman's Laws (The Physicists of Evolution)
*   **Position:** **Empirical Laws**.
*   **Nature:** Lehman's Laws (e.g., *Continuing Change*, *Increasing Complexity*) are formulated like laws of thermodynamics. They describe the behavior of "E-Type" (Evolutionary) systems.
*   **Consensus:** These are accepted as close to "scientific laws" as SE gets, observing that systems must evolve or decay.
*   **Relevance:** The **Collider** is essentially a machine to verify Lehman's laws. By tracking `unified_analysis.json` over time (drift), you are measuring the "Increasing Complexity" described by Lehman.

#### 8. Empirical Software Engineering (Basili)
*   **Position:** **Experimental Science**.
*   **Method:** Victor Basili (University of Maryland/NASA) pioneered the "Experience Factory" and **GQM (Goal-Question-Metric)** approach.
*   **Argument:** SE becomes science only when we observe, measure, and validate hypotheses with data, rather than relying on guru wisdom.
